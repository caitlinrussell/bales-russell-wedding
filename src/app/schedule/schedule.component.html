  • If you haven't been to Lincoln before, then welcome! These were some of our favorite places to eat, drink, and hang out in the city where we first met.

    Eat

    Ivanna Cone

    They have lines out the door for a reason! Lots of great seasonal flavors.

    Bison Witches

    Great lunch spot with... you guessed it... sandwiches! Also very addicting salsa con queso.

    Hub Cafe

    Fantastic place for brunch! They serve farm-to-table style meals in a casual setting and are located right on the Antelope Valley Trail.

    Honest Abe's Burgers & Freedom

    Lots of wild and crazy burger choices, plus boozy (and virgin) milkshakes.

    Grateful Bread

    These folks make insane mac and cheese! The flavors rotate, but you can find their flavor of the day on their Facebook page. They also have soup, sandwiches, and baked goods. But we were too busy eating the mac and cheese.

    The Oven

    Delicious Indian food, both downtown and in southeast Lincoln in an upscale setting. The downtown location also has a downstairs wine cellar.

    Runza

    If you haven't had a Runza yet, now's your chance! Enjoy the Nebraska classic at any of Lincoln's 14 convenient locations.

    And yes, there's even a subreddit dedicated to them.

    Misty's

    Misty's is a classic steakhouse in downtown, Havelock, and Williamsburg. They have a wide variety of dishes and make fantastic burgers. They also brew their own beer out of their downtown location.

    Drink

    The Mill

    Fantastic coffeehouse in downtown and College View.

    Starlite Lounge

    Retro cocktail lounge with very strong martinis and free apps on Friday afternoons.

    Barrymore's

    Classy bar at the back of a theater, famous for its Moscow Mules.

    Zipline Beer Hall

    Our favorite Lincoln brewery opened a brand-new beer hall and it's right by the wedding venue! This location offers a more relaxed location to enjoy their many handcrafted brews including several limited-edition and seasonal taps.

    Do

    Haymarket/The Railyard

    The historic Haymarket district houses many iconic Lincoln businesses and is a great area to walk through, whether you are looking to grab a quick snack, browse the book store, or read up on a bit of history. It has been extended to include the Railyard, a newly-developed district featuring shops, restaurants, and entertainment. Check out the football score on the giant screen, go ice skating, or grab a burger and a beer.

    Lincoln Children's Zoo

    Despite its name, this zoo is enjoyable for all ages.There are lots of great treasures here, from their headliner Amur leopard to a tribe of lemurs to giant tortoises. It is set amidst a very relaxing neighborhood in Lincoln and is next to Sunken Gardens.

    International Quilt Museum

    This museum, part of the University of Nebraska, has an extensive collection of over 5,000 quilts in a beautiful building near East Campus. They have several rotating exhibits of historical and modern quilts as well as a viewing room of their conservation efforts.

    Trails

    Whether you are looking to stroll with your toddler, take a walk through nature, or get a few laps in, there are many lovely multi-recreational trails in the city:

    • Antelope Valley Trail: new trail running alongside a creek with lots of art installations and play equipment near downtown
    • Holmes Lake: large park with 2.5 mile loop around the lake, dog park, and playground
    • Pioneers Park: expansive park with 8 miles of trails, varying habitats, and art installations
